# career-engine loop

## Principle
Do not ask first: how do I get hired faster

Ask first:
- what kind of work is actually happening here
- who has power
- what behavior is rewarded
- what kind of person gets worn down here
- what kind of person becomes stronger here

## Loop

### 1. Select a field site
A field site can be:
- one company
- one team family
- one manager archetype
- one role cluster
- one industry slice

### 2. Write the prior
Before gathering evidence, write:
- what you think this place is like
- why you think that
- what would prove you wrong

### 3. Gather signals
Sources:
- job descriptions
- org charts and LinkedIn traces
- hiring-manager posts
- employee interviews or podcasts
- product release notes
- technical blogs
- alumni and weak-tie conversations
- recruiter phrasing
- compensation shape
- wording around ownership, urgency, and cross-functional work

### 4. Split evidence from interpretation
For every note:
- evidence: exact thing observed
- interpretation: what you think it implies
- confidence: low / medium / high

### 5. Look for structure
Try to infer:
- promotion logic
- blame flow
- manager load
- degree of chaos
- strategy stability
- whether the team is a cost center, prestige center, or dumping ground

### 6. Decision
Classify each target:
- pursue
- explore carefully
- maintain weak tie only
- avoid

### 7. Reflection
After each real conversation or process step, update:
- what changed in your model
- what you had romanticized
- what you had underestimated
- what kind of future self this path seems to produce

## Default markdown files
- `profile.md`
- `work-model.md`
- `targets.md`
- `signals.md`
- `conversations.md`
- `decisions.md`

## Heuristic
A polished company story explains little.
Repeated concrete traces from workers, managers, and process friction explain much more.
