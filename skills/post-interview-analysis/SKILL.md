# post-interview-analysis

Use right after an interview or process step.

## Goal
Turn fresh interview impressions into structured evidence and a better decision.

## Inputs
- who the interview was with
- what was asked
- what felt easy, evasive, tense, or revealing
- what changed relative to the prior hypothesis
- any feedback received

## Output
Produce:
- what new signals appeared
- what prior hypothesis was confirmed or weakened
- likely hidden concerns from their side
- likely hidden risks from your side
- process read: healthy / mixed / concerning / unclear
- updated recommendation: continue / continue cautiously / pause / avoid
- next step

## Rules
- separate direct evidence from vibe
- treat polished answers as weak evidence unless backed by examples
- pay attention to omission, tension, inconsistency, and who owned which questions
- do not overfit to one unusually good or bad interviewer

## Minimal recipe
1. write the pre-interview hypothesis
2. record concrete moments from the interview
3. classify them as evidence, interpretation, or uncertainty
4. infer what the interview revealed about role, manager, team, and process
5. update the target decision

## Things to watch
- did they describe real work clearly or stay abstract
- did the manager sound overloaded, controlling, or absent
- did different interviewers tell the same story
- did they reveal success criteria for the role
- did they answer tradeoff questions honestly
- did the process itself feel respectful and well-shaped
